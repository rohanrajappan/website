CRT: Helios

Aerostructures Lead Engineer and Integration Lead

Led the aerodynamic design, mathematical simulation, and physical fabrication of a high-altitude rocket airframe structurally optimized to handle intense supersonic flight transitions up to a target altitude of 30,000 feet on a commercial motor. Managed a complex master CAD assembly and centralized parts directory detailing more than 100 individual physical components. Responsibilities included executing hands-on composite layout operations, configuring custom mold geometries, and conditioning fine surface finishes while establishing safety documentation protocols and training incoming team members.

Key Outcome: Implemented data-driven aerostructural design choices to optimize aerodynamic design. Leveraged background in mathematics and programming to solve boundary conditions on non-conical shapes. Led integration of launch vehicle from assembly to ignition. Created assembly and arming procedural checklists and guided a crew through assembly. Second author on technical paper that earned the maximum possible design and manufacturing scores from judging.
